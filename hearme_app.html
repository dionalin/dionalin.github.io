HearMe app
Gesture based audio recording experience

Overview

HearMe is an application on smartphone with efficient recording utilization and an audio exchange platform. The gesture based design offers a quicker access for sound capture and simplifies the interaction for the visually impaired.
Through HearMe, users are able to catch and review sounds in a snap by using simple gestures. In addition, HearMe helps users to organize all audio captures with ease, and further share the resource publicly to benefit the others. Plus, HearMe locates the user and provides nearby sound resources, such as historical stories, tour introductions, or recommendations. With all these exchanged information, users are able to get a better understanding of the new locations.
From the ease to capture sounds to further share the hearing experiences, HearMe empowers the visually impaired to interact with others and the world.

Impact

The visually impaired expressed their appreciation because we've come out a product that they wanted and needed. HearMe has brought a greater operational independence to the visually impaired, also reducing the mistakes and the burdens of the usability compared to the past.

Problem

285 million people are estimated to be visually impaired worldwide in 2014 ( WHO 2014 ) They’re seeking the aids of technologies to interact with the world, but technologies are normally expensive for them.
I played the role of UI/UX designer in HearMe, a design led project. A designer and a programmer working together, we started from the real needs and insights, and addressed this need by enabling people with visual impairments to connect to their environment and to the others by freely capturing and sharing the auditory information.

Design Process

Starting from two times of user researches and design proposals to iterative development, and finally two UX testings, the holistic UX process was to validate again and again and improve our design. Such approach helped us to better understand our users and the design itself.

User Study Insights

With purely solving the problems of our users at first, we wanted to create something convenient for them to use. We visited 5 visually impaired people. From there, we found some interesting insights.

I. Recording for the important moments everyday

During the study we started to realize that audio channel becomes their major sense in life, therefore they record everyday, including daily memo, class reviews, events or even the sound of the places they’ve been to. However, most of the information around them are based on visual form, such as infographics, icons, and banners. There is rarely any better ways for the visually impaired to acquire information around them.
What they do now is using recorders. However they always find the current ones troublesome, especially when they wanted to share or get the files back to listen to.

II. Missing the right timing to capture sounds

Besides, they also rely on smart phones with screen readers, here is an example of the Voiceover System on iOS. With the screen reader voice instruction, it could be a nice assistance for the visually impaired to use smartphones.

However, when the screen reader is on, it takes more time than visual selections, because the screen reader is based upon the GUI designs. For instance, the blind people have to swipe through items on the screen one by one, to know there’s a recording function for them, then decide whether they wanna activate or not. But Sounds are continuously flowing through by time. Thus a common problem is they usually miss the right timing to capture sounds they want.

III. More than recording, it’s life catching

For the visually impaired, the behavior is much more than recording, it’s a life catching, a memory keeping. They wanted to be able to reach and capture sounds in a snap and share their memories with their peers, just like we share photos online with our friends.

Design & Prototyping

To make them more connected to their lives by empowering their auditory experience, We’ve presented HearMe, a touch based app on mobile allowing them to keep and share their auditory memories in a very simple way.

In order to reduce the burden, we’ve proposed a touch-based interaction for them to do so, making the gestures correspond with the functionalities.

We've also developed a business canvus of how the product would last as a business. Sketches were scatched with gestures and sounds. And prototypes were made for usibility testings.

Implimentation

Gestures are the key interactions in the product spec, along with high contrast colors in design for the partially visually impaired.

I. Instant recording

Shortcut taps - with simple gestures, users are able to instantly initiate recording
Segments making - by right swiping, users could make segments to highlight important timings
Audio feedbacks - key actions provide significant voice feedbacks

II. Multiple search

Reference - users could search a record by using date, category or location

III. Fast browsing

Content preview - system auto-plays a brief of content to help users browse through items with ease

IV. Quick listening

Shortcut taps - corresponding with simple gestures when recording, users are able to instantly listening to recordings they want
Segments finding - users could listen to important timings just by transversely swiping
