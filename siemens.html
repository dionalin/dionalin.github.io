Sensis Siemens
Patient-medical-case panel

Overview

Sensis is a large device used in medical environment, connecting to a variety of software and hardware equipment, including cardiac waveform data acquisition and some inspecting equipments.
The User Experience Group (UX) at Siemens Corporate Research (SCR) in Princeton, NJ supports worldwide Siemens business units and their products.
To make doctor’s inspection process easier, we had improved the interactive design and user interface for Sensis.

Impact

With the old system, doctors needed to spend much time to proceed the inspection. After our redesign, the newer interface dramatically helps doctors reduce the time to read and to make decisions inspection processes for patients.

Usability testing

From the usability testing, we found that, once starting the system, many were unsure where to begin to accomplish the task, and were also overloaded by the information. Besides, they felt stressed out from the steps required.

Goal

“To reduce doctor’s burden of diagnosis, and accelerate the time when they make decisions”

Imagine such scenario, when encountering an emergency case, a doctor should be able to find the right information on the platform and make a decision for the next phase in a snap.

Information Archetecture

Based on our findings, we reorganized the critical flows of the process :

  1. Understanding the history of the patients
  2. Conducting the task
  3. Then tracking the real-time events

Design & Communication

Design, Product manager, and med-experts were in different time zones. For designer decisions, communication was the key to help project proceed. I made explorations in various design proposals, and tried learning from the feedbacks of stakeholders. A interesting way was, asking them what they think the icons are designed for before I told them what the icons were designer for. Which helps reducing the gap between design and user intuition.

Design highlights

I. Affordance of information

The running log was critical section for doctors. When it comes to a new patient, this is where a doctor understands the patient’s situation. The old system out of color plan that resulted in the low efficiency of using it.
After iterations, the final proposal coming up with a prioritized information view. It makes the amount of prescription, comments or treatments stand out from the platform, because those are the customized information for different treatments. Also, the mandatory information that they might had forgotten, would present alerts as a reminder.

II. Sustainable Design Spec

Medical product, in general, takes a long time at each level of process. After being designed and produced, a government audit is necessary, along with relevant localization. The effort goes on in each country and may take years. As the upstream design proposer, simplifying the design specifications is fairly important to such product life cycle.
The final UI elements were flexible when it come to different languages, which holds the design sustainability in the long process of production and could be expanded globally.

Impact

With the old system, doctors needed to spend much time to proceed the inspection. After our redesign, the feedbacks from the medical expert was pretty positive. The newer interface dramatically helps doctors reduce the time to read and to make decisions inspection processes for patients.

Final thoughts

This project made me realize how improtant design is to help people’s life easier. Doctor is one of the most stressful career life in general. A good design is magnificant to proceed a better medical decision, which brings a better quality medical treatment.
