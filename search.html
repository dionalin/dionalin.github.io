Search
Efficient Grocery Shopping Experience

Overview

When people do online grocery shopping, Search enables people to explore and find what they want within seconds. In Honestbee Search project, I recognised users' problems from user studies, reorganize the information architecture plan, and took the initiative to work with engineers as a small srum team. After redesiging, prototyping, and proposed a better search experience to team members.

Impact

People used to have irrelevant search experience on Honestbee. After the product improvement, we saw positive data in search feature. It helped increased engagement and conversion, and decreases frictions and shopping time. The move not only improved search result quality with measurable impacts, but also made the whole shopping experience better.

Problems

I. Friction of exploration

Users don’t know what products they are able to find on honestbee. Search was only available inside stores.

II. Relevant search result

When a user search for a simple term, such as "bread", "cheese", "milk" and "vegetables", he/she would see irrelevant results on top, such as flour (for bread), cheese cookies (for cheese), ice cream (for milk) and dog food (for vegetables).

Goal

To improve search experience and make relevant information accessible, and to help users shop faster.

User Study

In order to understand how people shop on Honestbee, I conducted user researches around the topic in different countries, which includes contextual Inquiry & task analysis with 15 honestbee users, to understand the similarity and differences between countries in South East Asia.
The analysis inculdes a report of buyer segments, and 2x2 mappings of motivation axis.

Findings

I. For ime Keepers

The key is to find what they've planned in mind as fast as possible. Thus Search needs to be as efficient and precise as possible

II. For Adventurers

Adventures are opened to linger around, and don't mind finding things that are similar to what they look for. Thus Search needs to help them explore relevant products that inspires their shopping intent

Main flow

Design Highlight

I. Emphasize content, remove clutters

After user testing sessions and iterations, we've found that icons doesn't help differentiate items, yet content does help. So we removed icons, and adjust the color and the typography in the UI, to let content stand out.

II. Auto-complete with contextual information

Auto-complete includes stores, top products in stores, and popular product terms. For people who comes to find a store they like, or who comes to find the product inventory in different stores, or who wants to explore what products are available in Honestbee.

III. Avoid price comparison across stores

Global search result emphasizes the product inventory in stores without product prices, to avoid price competition between stores

IV. Encourage in-store shopping order

The in-store search was designed to encourage people shop from a single store in an order, to avoid paying for multiple delivery and concierge fee.

V. Cross-sell when the lack of inventory in the store

When people can't find products in a store they are shopping in, a make-up model comes in. This not only helps cross-sell products, but also decreases the sense of loss in people

Algorithm Improvement

Other than the UI design, the search result was poor in showing relevant result for users.
In order to provide better search result, we made the hypothesis that if a search term equals to a category name, he/she need to see all the products under the category. A benefit of this is even a product doesn't has the same name as the category, he/she then wouldn't miss the product, e.g., Golden Brie is a kinda of cheese, if user search for cheese, he/she wouldn't miss the product anymore.

Result Quality Compare

After the information architecture improvement in the algorithm, search result is a lot more precise and quality.
